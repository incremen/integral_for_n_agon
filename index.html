The Integral n-gon

A regular polygon emerges from pure numerical integration — no geometry tricks.

Controls

The Equation

$$z(X) = \int_0^X \exp\!\left(i \cdot \frac{2\pi}{n} \cdot \sum_{k=1}^{n} \frac{1}{1 + e^{-\theta(x-k)}}\right) dx$$

How It Works

The Setup

We integrate a complex-valued function. The result is a parametric curve in the complex plane — the horizontal axis is the real part, the vertical axis is the imaginary part, and the integration variable $x$ acts as time driving a pen across the canvas.

Constant Speed

The integrand is $e^{i \cdot \text{phase}}$, which always has magnitude 1. So the pen moves at a constant speed — it can only change direction, never accelerate or slow down.

Sharpness ($\theta$)

Inside the exponent, the sum of sigmoids acts like a staircase. Each time $x$ passes an integer $k$, the sigmoid $\frac{1}{1+e^{-\theta(x-k)}}$ flips from 0 to 1, bumping the total sum by 1. This causes the phase to jump by $2\pi/n$ — a sudden change in direction that creates a corner of the polygon.

$\theta$ controls how steep each step is. Low $\theta$ gives a gradual, rounded turn. High $\theta$ makes the step nearly instantaneous, producing a sharp corner.
