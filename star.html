$$z(x) = \int_0^x \exp\!\left( i \sum_{k=1}^{2n} c_k \cdot \frac{1}{1 + e^{-(y-k)\theta}} \right) dy$$

↑ Same idea as the n-gon, but with $2n$ sigmoids and alternating turn angles $c_k$.
Odd $k$ (outer points): $c_k = \frac{\pi}{n}(1 + s)$. Even $k$ (inner points): $c_k = \frac{\pi}{n}(1 - s)$.
$s = 0$ gives a regular $2n$-gon; $s = 2$ gives a classic $n$-pointed star.
